This is a screening tool, not a diagnosis. The DASS-21 measures how strongly you've experienced symptoms relative to a general population — it can't diagnose anything. If your scores feel meaningful, or if anything you read here resonates uncomfortably, talking with a clinician, counsellor, or trusted person is a good next step.

About this tool. This is the DASS-21 (Depression Anxiety Stress Scales — 21 item version) by S.H. Lovibond & P.F. Lovibond (1995), University of New South Wales / Psychology Foundation of Australia. The DASS is in the public domain. The 21 items, scoring procedure, and severity bands here are reproduced unchanged from the canonical version published at www2.psy.unsw.edu.au/dass.

How scoring works. Each subscale (Depression, Anxiety, Stress) sums seven items scored 0–3, then doubles the total so the score (0–42) is comparable with the original DASS-42 norms. Severity bands come from the Lovibond & Lovibond manual.
